Local School Wins Regional Science Fair Competition

By: Sarah Mitchell | Published: August 25, 2025 | Category: School & Sports

Students from Riverbend Elementary School celebrate their first-place finish at the regional science fair. Photo by Mike Johnson.

Riverbend Elementary School's science team brought home the gold medal from this weekend's regional competition, showcasing innovative projects in renewable energy and environmental conservation that impressed judges and fellow competitors alike.

The team, consisting of 12 fourth and fifth-grade students, competed against 25 other schools from across the region in the annual Regional Elementary Science Fair held at the Convention Center downtown.

"I'm incredibly proud of these students," said Mrs. Jennifer Adams, the school's science teacher who coached the team. "They've been working on their projects since the beginning of the school year, and their dedication really shows in the quality of their work."

Winning Projects

The team's success was led by three standout projects that each earned individual recognition:

"Solar-Powered Water Purification System" by fifth-grader Emma Thompson took first place in the Environmental Science category. Her project demonstrated how simple solar technology could be used to purify water in areas without access to clean drinking water.

"Wind Power for Rural Areas" by fourth-grader Marcus Williams earned second place in the Renewable Energy category. Marcus built a working model of a wind turbine and calculated how much electricity it could generate for a small farm.

"Composting for Beginners" by fifth-grader Ava Johnson received the Innovation Award for her project showing how families can reduce waste through home composting.

School Support

Principal Dr. Robert Chen praised the collaborative effort that made this success possible. "This achievement reflects not just the hard work of our students, but also the support of our teachers, parents, and the entire school community," he said.

The school's Parent-Teacher Association helped fund materials for the projects, while local businesses including Thompson's Hardware Store and Green Valley Nursery donated supplies and expertise.

Looking Ahead

The winning team will now advance to the state science fair competition scheduled for October 15-16 in the capital city. They will compete against the top elementary schools from across the entire state.

"We're excited about the state competition, but we're also focused on inspiring more students to get involved in science," said Mrs. Adams. "Win or lose, these students have already achieved something amazing."

The school is planning a celebration assembly next week to honor the team's achievement and encourage other students to participate in next year's science fair.
